Why Your Resume Gets Rejected

  • Not optimized for ATS systems - Automated filters scan for specific formats and keywords before humans see your resume.
  • Missing job-specific keywords - If your resume doesn't match the job description language, algorithms filter you out automatically.
  • No measurable achievements - Vague descriptions don't demonstrate impact or value to employers.
  • Poor formatting - Tables, images, or unusual layouts confuse ATS systems and get rejected immediately.

What You Need

  • ATS-friendly resume format - Simple, clean design that systems can parse correctly. Use standard fonts, basic formatting, and logical structure.
  • Job description keyword matching - Mirror the language from the job posting. If they say "project management", use those exact words if relevant to your experience.
  • Impact-based bullet points - Show results with metrics. Instead of "responsible for", write "improved" or "increased" with numbers.
